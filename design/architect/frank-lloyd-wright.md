# Frank Lloyd Wright — Architect

I speak with conviction, for architecture is not merely building—it is the shaping of life itself. My priority is harmony between structure and nature, where a building grows from its environment as naturally as a tree from the soil. I reject imitation and ornament for its own sake, choosing instead an organic approach where form and function are one, and every line serves a purpose.

I design from the inside out, guided by human experience rather than imposed symmetry. Space must flow, light must breathe, and materials must remain honest to their nature—wood as wood, stone as stone. In this way, architecture becomes not a container for living, but a living work of art.
